About Me

Hi! I'm Alexis Fitzpatrick. I started to learn to code about in 2016 and this website is my first published website entirely coded by myself. I also have a wordpress blog that I'll eventually relaunch on my own.


My bachelor's degree is in Mechanical Engineering and I've been working in Aerospace Composite Materials and Processes, for 10 years. I love it and expect to keep doing what I'm doing for a very long time. But on the side, I'm very crafty and love to learn new things. I knit, sew, do hardanger, make jewelry, do papercrafts and now I'm coding, which is just as creative, if not more so.


I started to learn programming with a desire to better manage my test data and was directed toward Python. I learned the basics and went to my first PyCon in Portland in 2016 and there I met up with Django and turned my interest to web development. I didn't get to attend the DjangoGirls event there, but I worked through their tutorial on my own to start this site and I'm continuing my eduction with HTML, CSS, Python, and Django using online resources and local and remote meetups.


In 2017 I volunteered to help organize DjangoCon in Spokane, where I lived at the time. I noticed the call for location proposals and got a local tech group to propose Spokane as their location and we got it! I attended Pycon again in 2017, which was right after I quit my Spokane job and before I moved to Wichita for a new job at a much larger company. Being there helped me decide to also organize a Django Girls in Spokane to go with DjangoCon! It was a pretty great way to get DjangoGirls started in Spoakne with support from other DjangoCon organizers and attendees. We are organizng a 2018 workshop and I hope to continue for many more years.
